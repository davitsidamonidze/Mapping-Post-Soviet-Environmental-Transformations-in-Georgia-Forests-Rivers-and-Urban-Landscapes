# Mapping-Post-Soviet-Environmental-Transformations-in-Georgia-Forests-Rivers-and-Urban-Landscapes
Datasets:
Satellite Imagery: Landsat (1990–2022) and Sentinel-2 (2015–2025) used to assess forest cover change and urban expansion.
Historical Maps: Soviet-era topographic maps (1:50,000 scale) digitized for land use comparison.
Hydrochemical Records: River water quality data from Ministry of Environmental Protection of Georgia (2000–2023) for Iori and Alazani basins.
Urban & Demographic Data: Population density and urban growth statistics for Tbilisi and secondary cities.
Climate Data: Temperature, precipitation, and extreme event records from Georgian National Environment Agency.
Tools & Methods:
GIS: QGIS and ArcGIS for mapping land cover change, urban sprawl, and river network alterations.
Remote Sensing Analysis: Python with rasterio and earthpy libraries for vegetation indices (NDVI, EVI) and land cover classification.
Spatial Analysis: Python (pandas, geopandas, networkx) to analyze relationships between industrial sites, population density, and environmental degradation.
Interactive Visualizations: Folium and Plotly for online, interactive maps of forest loss, river pollution, and urban expansion.
Data Integration: Combining remote sensing, historical maps, and statistical records into a unified GIS database.
Key Findings:
Significant forest cover decline in Samegrelo and Atchara regions post-1991, correlated with land privatization and agricultural expansion.
River degradation in the Iori and Alazani basins due to industrial collapse and lack of wastewater management.
Rapid urban expansion in Tbilisi, with peripheral informal settlements contributing to pollution and landscape fragmentation.
Potential Biases & Limitations:
Satellite imagery gaps for early 1990s may reduce temporal precision.
Historical map digitization introduces potential georeferencing errors.
Limited hydrochemical data for smaller rivers may underestimate pollution trends.
